WIND RIVER KNOWLEDGE FORUM

General / Introductory

What is Wind River Knowledge Forum:

The Wind River Knowledge Forum is one of several Support Tools available to Wind River Customers.

The Forum has been designed and implemented to be a tool that allows customers to search for solutions to common problems, ask certain types of questions and to contribute to the knowledge base by answering questions.

The Wind River Knowledge Forum is not intended to be a Discussion Forum -  questions that require considerable back-and-forth transactions, or in-depth investigations, should be routed to one of Wind River’s other support tools.

It is important to note that in addition to customer questions, Wind River Customer Support Engineers actively post Question/Answer pairs based on experience gained during traditional support of products and tribal knowledge.  Over time the Knowledge Forum should become a significant knowledge repository of Problem/Solutions Pairs.

It is available at ask.windriver.com.

Please be Aware:
The Wind River Knowledge Forum is an open Support Repository that is accessible to Wind River Employees and customers that have active Support Maintenance Agreements.   

Questions posted are visible to all users and all users can contribute to the answer. Both Questions and Answers can be edited by every user on Wind River Knowledge Forum.

Users should refrain from posting proprietary or confidential information on Wind River Knowledge Forum. Wind River System is not responsible for ensuring the privacy of data on Wind River Knowledge Forum.

Code of conduct:

Given the open nature of the Wind River Knowledge Forum, proprietary information should not be posted on the forum.  If Wind River Customer Support requires proprietary information, it will be requested via an off-forum communication.

The Wind River Knowledge Forum is intended to be a self-moderating vehicle with minimum moderation by Wind River.   Given this assumption, it is expected that each user will treat others with tolerance, respect and professionalism.

This Forum is not a platform to argue over information or point out faults of others.  If a user sees inaccurate, misleading or malicious information – the content can be voted down or flagged it as offensive.  Voting down content moves it down in the knowledge value tree. Transactions marked as offensive trigger a review by the forum administrator.

Who manages the Wind River Knowledge Forum:

Wind River Customer Support Engineers are the primary moderators of the Wind River Knowledge Forum. However, customers can answer posts and may earn Moderator Level Privileges based on karma points earned by actively participating in the Wind River Knowledge Forum. The goal is allow experienced users to edit the information to improve the overall quality and accuracy of the Wind River Knowledge Forum content.

What Other support channels are available to customers:

The Wind River Knowledge Forum is one of a number of Wind River Customer Support vehicles. Since this Forum is intended for a subset of possible customer issues, users may be directed to support services other than the Wind River Knowledge Forum.

The Wind River Knowledge Forum is not the best choice for the following:

  • Content that contains confidential or proprietary information
  • ITAR classified information
  • Problems that cannot be categorized into a concise Question/Answer Pair
  • Problems that require more than a few back-and-forth interactions
  • Problems that require significant debugging effort or investigation
  • Problems that are time-critical

Issues that do not qualify as good candidates for the Wind River Knowledge Forum may be routed to other Wind River Support Services.

All Wind River Support Service pages contain a common Support Service Selector across the top of the service’s home page.


The services available are:

  • Support Home
  • Wind River Knowledge Forum
  • On Demand Learning
  • TSR Manager

The Support Home selection opens a web page to the Wind River main Support Portal – Online Support (OLS).  OLS contains an extensive set of tools and services:

  • TechTips and Application Notes
  • Product Documentation
  • Service Packs and Patches
  • BSP downloads
  • Firmware Updates
  • On-Chip Debugging Register Files
  • Live Chat
  • License Information
  • Etc.

 

On-Demand Learning is a new service that contains training material and videos.  

Wind River’s primary Support Service is available as a Technical Support Request (TSR).   TSR’s can be filed via the TSR Manager selection.   

Customers who have confidential, time-critical questions and/or issues that do not qualify for a Wind River Knowledge Forum Question/ Answer, should file a conventional TSR.  

If an issue filed on the Wind River Knowledge Forum becomes out-of-scope for the forum, the user may be directed to one of the other Wind River Support Services.

Asking Questions

Before posting a question search for an existing solution:

Before posting a question on the Wind River Knowledge Forum, users should search the Forum for possible solutions to the same or similar issue.  

Searching can be done via keyword phrases, tag searches or a combination of the two.

As the Wind River Knowledge Forum grows over time, the odds of finding an existing solution increases so it’s important to spend a bit of time searching for known problem/solution pairs.   Even if a solution is not found, searches usually result in gained knowledge and understanding of related issues.

What types of questions should be asked:

After the Wind River Knowledge Forum has been searched unsuccessfully for a solution, the user should determine if the question is of the type intended for the Forum.   

Wind River Knowledge Forum is intended for specific type of questions – primarily questions that meet the following criteria:

  • Can be categorized into a concise Question/Answer pair
  • Does not contain confidential or proprietary information
  • Non-Subjective
  • Problems that do not require more than a few back-and-forth iterations
  • Problems that do not require significant debug or investigation
  • Problems that are not time-critical

Open ended subjective questions, which require discussions and debugging, are not best suited for Wind River Knowledge Forum.

If the problem is time-critical it may be best to file an official Technical Support Request (TSR). This can be done using the TSR Manager link on the forum page.

Questions about the Wind River Knowledge Forum site itself, or other Wind River websites, should be avoided. A feedback mechanism is provided on each page as a way to communicate suggestions or problems with the Wind River Knowledge Forum to Wind River.

What information should be provided when posting a question:

After an unsuccessful search for an existing solution, and qualifying the question as a good candidate for the Wind River Knowledge Forum, a new question can be posted to the Wind River Knowledge Forum.

When posting to the Wind River Knowledge Forum, the user will need to supply enough information in order for Wind River Customer Support, and possibly non-Wind River personnel, to answer the question.   The format of the Question on the Forum includes the following fields:

  • Question Title Line
  • Question Description
  • Tags
Question’s title line:
Wind River has hundreds of products, each with thousands of possible questions.  Wind River Customer Support has dedicated Customer Support Engineers (CSE) assigned to each product area.  No one Customer Support Engineer could know all products so it’s important to word the question title effectively.

In addition to finding a possible solution,  searching the Wind River Knowledge Forum, prior to posting a question, gives the user a sense of how question titles should be worded.  Posts with high ratings are usually best to review for proper format, although, this is not necessarily true for all cases.

Question description:
The Question Description is obviously the most important part of submitting an issue on the Wind River Knowledge Forum.  In addition to supplying a clear and concise description of the actual problem, it is very important to supply enough information such as errors, version info, etc on the product or function the problem is applicable to.

What constitutes a good Problem Description is very subjective.  It is important to remember that too little or non-concise information may cause misinterpretation of the problem, delay in responses, additional back-and-forth inquiries and possibly Wind River Customer Support Engineer asking submitter that a Technical Support Request (TSR) be created to receive further assistance on the issue.

The Wind River Knowledge Forum uses tags extensively. 

When posting a question to the Wind River Knowledge Forum, tags should be used to identify the product area the question applies to. The tagging facility uses tag trees that assist the user in selecting the correct tags.   Use as many tags as necessary to properly categorize the question.

More details About Tags are described in a separate section of this document.

Answering Questions

Who are contributors:

Anyone participating on the Wind River Knowledge Forum is a contributor. Wind River Knowledge Forum Questions can be answered by any user on the Forum.  Typically, questions will be answered by Wind River Customer Support (CSE) but due to the open nature of the Wind River Knowledge Forum, answers may also be submitted by other users.

Wind River Knowledge Forum Answers format:

Wind River Knowledge Forum questions may often have more than one answer. When submitting another possible answer to a forum question, the answer should be a separate entry on the forum – i.e. by filling in the “Your Answer” section and by hitting the Post Your Answer button. Similarly, always vote on the best or most precise Answer so that relevant Answers are more visible to other users.

Comments
In addition to posting separate transactions for a question, each transaction has a “Post a Comment” option. Comments can be used when asking for or providing additional information to a specific transaction and as a short discussion mechanism. 

Keep in mind when answering a question:

Much of the information is intuitive and also may have been mentioned in previous sections of this guide.

  • The Wind River Knowledge Forum is designed to exchange information that is not propriety in nature
  • It is an open platform and other customers and Wind River employees have access to every article available on Wind River Knowledge Forum.
  • One of the possible answers to a question which is informational in nature. Several possible answers can be added to a single question.
  • Existing ‘answer’ should be edited for completeness or to provide additional/ accurate information.
  • Answer should be limited to delivering the information requested and not to ask follow-up questions as they tend to dilute the essence of questions and answers.
  • For brief discussions on a question please use the commenting facility.
  • Wind River System reserves the right to remove Question/ Answer articles and contributors from Wind River Knowledge Forum.
  • It is expected that each contributor on Wind River Knowledge Forum will treat others with tolerance, respect and professionalism.
  • Wind River Knowledge Forum is not a platform to argue over the information; if you see misinformation or inaccurate information, vote it down. You always have the option to edit and improve the existing questions and answers.

Users have option to decide the content that they want to be displayed. On the right side of the Wind River Knowledge Forum, there are various selections to choose from. Users can choose to subscribe or ignore certain tags. Similarly, tag search is available as well.

Tags:
Wind River Knowledge Forum provides an extensive list of tags for each of its product lines and their sub categories. This makes it easy for users to tag their questions and make sure that tags are used consistently across the Forum. If your Question/ Answer doesn’t match any of the tags that are provided, you can add a custom tag to your question which be added to the tag list if and when it is approved by the moderator. If the tag is not approved, the moderator will add an existing relevant tag to your question. Similarly, you can remove a tag though this is not recommended. Please make sure to add all related tags from each level of tags that are available for your question.

 

Other Features in Knowledge Forum

How does karma system work?

When a question or answer is upvoted, the user who posted it will gain some points, which are called \"karma points\". These points serve as a rough measure of the community’s trust in him/her. Various moderation tasks are gradually assigned to users based on these points.
For example, if you ask an interesting question or give a helpful answer, your input will be upvoted. On the other hand if the answer is misleading - it will be downvoted. Each vote in favor will generate 10 points, each vote against will subtract 10 points. There is a limit of 200 points that can be accumulated for a question or answer per day. How to change my picture (gravatar) and what is gravatar?

The picture that appears on the users profiles is called gravatar (which means globally recognized avatar).
Here is how it works: a cryptographic key (unbreakable code) is calculated from your email address. You upload your picture (or your favorite alter ego image) to the website gravatar.com from where we later retrieve your image using the key.
This way all the websites you trust can show your image next to your posts and your email address remains private.
Please personalize your account with an image - just register at gravatar.com (please be sure to use the same email address that you used to register with Wind River Systems). The default image which looks like a kitchen tile is generated automatically.

Flagging a content offensive:
Users have the choice to flag content offensive. Any article which receives 3 offensive flags will be removed from the Wind River Knowledge Forum. Wind River Knowledge Forum administrator can remove any article at any time, if he/ she finds it offensive for general community.
